# Evidence Synthesis: Effectiveness of Palliative Care in End-Stage Liver Disease

This repository showcases the complete systematic review workflow 
undertaken as part of a published protocol in **JBI Evidence Synthesis 
(2025)**. The review evaluates the effectiveness of palliative care for 
managing end-stage liver disease (ESLD), focusing on quality of life, 
quality of care, symptom burden, and patient satisfaction.

> **Published Protocol:** Manjunath H, Manoj A, et al. *Effectiveness of 
> palliative care for the management of end-stage liver disease: a 
> systematic review protocol.* JBI Evidence Synthesis 2025; 23(10):2104–2112.  
> **PROSPERO Registration:** CRD42024586855

---

## Project Overview

End-stage liver disease affects approximately 2 million people annually 
worldwide. Despite its significant symptom burden, palliative care remains 
underutilised in this population. This systematic review was designed to 
address that gap by synthesising evidence across multiple study designs, 
settings, and palliative care approaches — following **JBI methodology** 
and reported according to **PRISMA 2020** standards.

The review followed a pre-registered protocol and involved multi-database 
searching, independent dual-reviewer screening, standardised critical 
appraisal, and structured data extraction across 15 included studies 
spanning 2006–2024.

The peer-reviewed and published systematic review protocol, accepted in 
*JBI Evidence Synthesis* (2025). It outlines the full methodological 
framework for the review, including:
- PICO-structured review question
- Eligibility criteria (participants, interventions, comparators, outcomes)
- Multi-database search strategy (PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane, CINAHL, 
  Scopus, Web of Science, and grey literature sources)
- Study selection, data extraction, and synthesis plan (meta-analysis 
  and SWiM narrative synthesis)
- Risk of bias and GRADE certainty of evidence approach

### 3. Search Strategies
**File:** `[PRISMA_Flow_Chart](https://github.com/harshithaManjunath-ux/EVIDENCE-SYNTHESIS/blob/main/PROJECT%20FOLDER/2)%20SEARCH%20STRATEGIES.pdf)`

Comprehensive, reproducible database search strategies were developed using 
**Medical Subject Headings (MeSH)** and Boolean operators across 11 
databases and grey literature sources. The search was conducted in August 
2024 without date restrictions to ensure full coverage of the evidence base. 
Databases searched include:

PubMed · EMBASE (Ovid) · Cochrane CENTRAL · CINAHL (EBSCOhost) · 
Scopus · Web of Science · Google Scholar · ProQuest · MedNar · 
WHO · Shodhganga · Clinicaltrials.gov

### 4. PRISMA Flow Chart
**File:** `[PRISMA_Flow_Chart](https://github.com/harshithaManjunath-ux/EVIDENCE-SYNTHESIS/blob/main/PROJECT%20FOLDER/4)%20PRISMA%20FLOW%20CHART.pdf)`

### 5. Critical Appraisal
**File:** `[Critical_Appraisal](https://github.com/harshithaManjunath-ux/EVIDENCE-SYNTHESIS/blob/main/PROJECT%20FOLDER/5)%20CRITICAL%20APPRAISAL%20SUMMARIES.pdf)` 

Methodological quality assessment of all included studies using 
standardised **JBI critical appraisal tools**, selected according to study 
design:

- **ROB2** — Revised Cochrane tool for randomised controlled trials
- **ROBINS-I** — Risk of bias for non-randomised studies of interventions
- **JBI Checklists** — For cohort studies, analytical cross-sectional 
  studies, and non-RCTs

Each study was independently appraised by two reviewers, with discrepancies 
resolved by a third reviewer. Results are presented in tabular format with 
a narrative summary, in line with JBI methodology.

### 6. Data Extraction Table
**File:** `[Data_Extraction_Table_MAIN.xlsx](https://github.com/harshithaManjunath-ux/EVIDENCE-SYNTHESIS/blob/main/PROJECT%20FOLDER/6)%20DATA%20EXTRACTION.xlsx)`

A structured data extraction table covering all 15 included studies, 
developed using the standardised JBI SUMARI extraction framework and 
modified for this review. For each study, the table captures:

- Study characteristics (design, country, single/multi-centre, 
  randomisation, blinding, confounders)
- Population details (inclusion/exclusion criteria, sample size, 
  demographics)
- Intervention and comparator details (LTAD, LVP, alfapump, palliative 
  care consultation, TIPS, nurse-led care, tunnelled peritoneal catheters)
- Outcome data across all four domains: Quality of Life · Quality of Care · 
  Symptom Burden · Patient Satisfaction
- Reviewer comments and funding sources
